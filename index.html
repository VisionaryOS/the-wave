Data Centers Are Driving a US Gas Boom

Data centers have nearly tripled the demand for gas-fired power in the US over the past two years, according to Global Energy Monitor. Gas projects explicitly linked to data centers increased by almost 25 times since 2024 - from 4 gigawatts to over 97 gigawatts. If all projects in the development pipeline are built, it would add 252 gigawatts to the US gas fleet, increasing it by nearly 50%.

The findings come as the Trump administration encourages data center build-out while rolling back pollution regulations. Beyond CO2 emissions from burning gas, experts worry about methane leaks during extraction - methane is 80 times more potent than CO2 over 20 years. The administration recently extended deadlines for regulations requiring operators to track and stop methane leaks. As one analyst noted: "AI is not going away. It's really a question of what can we do to diminish the impact."
