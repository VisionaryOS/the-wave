The EU AI Act Takes Effect: What Changes for Tech Companies

After years of deliberation, the European Union's landmark AI Act has entered its enforcement phase, creating the world's most comprehensive regulatory framework for artificial intelligence. Companies deploying AI systems in Europe now face strict transparency and accountability requirements.

The legislation introduces a risk-based classification system, with "high-risk" applications in healthcare, law enforcement, and education subject to mandatory auditing. Tech giants have scrambled to establish compliance teams, while some smaller AI startups are reconsidering their European market strategies entirely.

AI Discovers New Antibiotic Class in Record Time

Researchers at MIT and Harvard have announced the discovery of a novel class of antibiotics, identified through an AI system that screened over 12 million chemical compounds in just three weeks. The discovery represents a potential breakthrough against drug-resistant bacteria.

Traditional drug discovery processes typically span 10-15 years and cost billions. The AI approach compressed initial screening to weeks, with the most promising candidates already entering pre-clinical trials. Medical experts call this a "paradigm shift" in pharmaceutical research methodology.

A coalition of researchers, startups, and tech giants has released "Foundation-7," a state-of-the-art open source AI model that rivals commercial offerings. The release signals a fundamental shift in how advanced AI capabilities are distributed and accessed globally.
